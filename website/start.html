Getting started

Four steps to a stove that watches itself.

No tools, no codes to copy. Tap to pair and you're done.

  1. Get the app

    Hestia is a web app — nothing to download from a store. Add it to your home screen so it opens like any other app, or just use it in your browser.

  2. Create your account

    Sign up with your email. Then create a household — or join one a family member already set up. The person who creates it is the admin.

  3. Pair this device

    Tap Pair this device below. Hestia recognises the unit you tapped and adds it to your household. If it already belongs to someone, you can request access.

  4. Set it and relax

    Invite the rest of the household, tune the safety thresholds for how you cook, and you're set. From now on the kitchen watches itself — on the device, not the cloud.

Get the app

Open Hestia, then add it to your home screen.

Hestia is a Progressive Web App: install it for one-tap access, or use it right in the browser. No app store, no waiting.

Open Hestia & pair
iPhone & iPad You're likely here
  1. 1Open Hestia in Safari.
  2. 2Tap the Share button (the square with an up-arrow).
  3. 3Choose Add to Home Screen, then Add.
  4. 4Open Hestia from your home screen — it runs full-screen, like an app.
Android You're likely here
  1. 1Open Hestia in Chrome.
  2. 2Tap the ⋮ menu in the top-right.
  3. 3Choose Install app (or Add to Home screen).
  4. 4Confirm Install — Hestia appears with your other apps.

Installing is optional. Everything works in the browser too — installing just makes it one tap away and enables notifications.

Good to know

A few things before you start.

Safety never waits on Wi-Fi

Detection, the warning, and the auto-shutoff all run on the device in your kitchen. The app is the window onto it — not the brain behind it.

No cameras

Presence is sensed locally. Nothing streams your kitchen anywhere, and your status and history stay with your household.

Already someone's device?

If this unit is already paired, pairing will offer to request access. An admin of that household approves you as a member.
